About Our Mission

Leveraging technology to build a safer, more resilient urban environment for the citizens of Delhi.

From Data to Decision

Urban flooding is a critical challenge facing metropolitan areas worldwide. In a city as dynamic as Delhi, seasonal monsoons can quickly overwhelm infrastructure, leading to widespread disruption and posing significant risks to public safety.

Our project, FloodWatch, was born from a desire to apply technology for civic good. We aim to bridge the gap between raw data and effective disaster response by providing city authorities with a centralized, intuitive, and real-time command center.

By transforming complex sensor data into a clear visual interface with actionable intelligence, we empower decision-makers to act faster, allocate resources more efficiently, and ultimately protect lives and property.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the water level data collected?

Our system is designed to integrate with real-time data from IoT sensors deployed in critical flood-prone areas across the city. For this prototype, we are using a simulation to generate dynamic data every 10 seconds.

Is the dashboard accessible on mobile devices?

Yes, the entire dashboard is fully responsive. It is designed to be accessible and functional on desktops, tablets, and mobile phones, ensuring that authorities can access critical information from anywhere.

What do the different alert levels (Danger, Warning, Safe) mean?

Danger (Red): Indicates severe waterlogging (>1.8m) requiring immediate emergency response. Warning (Orange): Indicates significant waterlogging (>0.9m) that disrupts traffic. Safe (Blue): Indicates minor or no waterlogging.

Who is the primary user of this dashboard?

The primary users are civic authorities, including disaster management agencies, traffic police, and municipal corporations. The goal is to provide them with a unified operational picture for a coordinated and efficient response. A simplified, public-facing version could be a future development.

How are the 'Actionable Directives' generated?

Currently, the directives are based on a predefined rules engine that links water levels and location types to standard operating procedures (SOPs). Future versions will incorporate a machine learning model to provide more dynamic and predictive recommendations.

What are the future plans for FloodWatch?

Our roadmap includes integrating live traffic data from APIs like Google Maps, developing a public alert system via SMS and WhatsApp, and implementing a predictive model using historical rainfall and flood data to forecast potential waterlogging events before they occur.
